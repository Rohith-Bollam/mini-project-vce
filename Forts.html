Red Fort

Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a historic fortress built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648. Made primarily of red sandstone, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The fort is a symbol of India's rich history, showcasing a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural styles. It played a pivotal role in India's independence movement and is where the Prime Minister hoists the national flag on Independence Day. Today, it stands as a UNESCO World Heritage site, attracting millions of visitors annually.

Things to do:

1.Admire the stunning architecture

2.Treat your eyes at the sight of the manicured gardens

3.Explore the Khas Mahal, Mumtaz Mahal and Rang Mahal

4.Watch the iconic Light and Sound Show

5.Shop at the local market

Visit :October to March

Address : Netaji Subhash Marg, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi – 110006

Timings : 9:30 AM – 4:30 PM, closed on Monday

Entry Fee : INR 35 (Indian Nationals), INR 500 (Foreign Nationals)

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort, located in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, is one of the largest and most magnificent forts in India. Built in 1459 by Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur, the fort stands atop a 400-foot hill and offers stunning views of the "Blue City" below. Its massive walls, intricate palaces, and expansive courtyards reflect Rajput architecture and craftsmanship. Inside, it houses a museum showcasing royal artifacts, armory, and paintings. Mehrangarh Fort is a symbol of Rajasthan's rich history and is a popular tourist attraction, renowned for its grandeur and cultural significance.

Gwalior Fort

Gwalior Fort

Gwalior Fort, located in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, is a historic hilltop fort renowned for its stunning architecture and strategic significance. Dating back to the 6th century, it has been ruled by various dynasties, including the Tomars, Mughals, and Marathas. The fort is famous for its massive sandstone walls, intricate palaces, temples, and water tanks. Notable structures include the Man Singh Palace, known for its blue-tiled facade, and the Teli Ka Mandir, an ancient Hindu temple. Often called "The Gibraltar of India," Gwalior Fort is a symbol of resilience and architectural grandeur.

Things to do:

3.Walk around Gujari Mahal, Karan Palace and Shah Jahan Mahal

4.Worship Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu at Teli ka Mandir

5.Admire the beauty of Sas Bahu Temple

Agra Fort

Agra Fort

Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Agra, India. Built by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in 1565, it served as the main residence of the Mughal rulers until 1638. The fort is a massive red sandstone structure spread over 94 acres and consists of several impressive buildings, including the Jahangir Palace, Khas Mahal, and the Diwan-i-Khas. Its architecture blends Islamic and Hindu styles, showcasing the grandeur of the Mughal Empire. Agra Fort played a crucial role in India’s history and offers stunning views of the nearby Taj Mahal.

Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Quila or the "Golden Fort," is a massive sandstone fortress located in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan. Built in 1156 by Rawal Jaisal, it stands majestically in the Thar Desert and is famous for its golden hue that glows under the sun. Unlike most forts, Jaisalmer Fort is a living fort, with residents still occupying its narrow streets, houses, temples, and shops. The fort showcases Rajput architecture with intricately carved havelis, Jain temples, and a royal palace. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of Rajasthan’s rich cultural and historical heritage.

Things to do:

3.Get an insight into Jaisalmer’s Heritage at the museum

4.Marvel at the sight of Laxminath Temple

6.Walk up to the Cannon Point

Kangra Fort

Kangra Fort

Kangra Fort, located in the Kangra Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India, is one of the oldest and largest forts in the country, with origins dating back to the Trigarta Kingdom, mentioned in the Mahabharata. Built by the Katoch dynasty, it sits strategically at the confluence of the Banganga and Majhi rivers. The fort has witnessed numerous invasions over centuries, including by Mahmud of Ghazni in the 11th century and Mughal Emperor Jahangir. Known for its massive stone walls, gates, and temples within its complex, Kangra Fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding valley and has deep historical and cultural significance.

Chittor Fort

Chittor Fort

Chittor Fort, located in Chittorgarh, Rajasthan, is one of the largest and most historically significant forts in India. It served as the capital of the Mewar kingdom and was built in the 7th century by the Maurya dynasty. The fort is known for its massive hilltop structure, covering around 700 acres, and houses several palaces, temples, towers, and reservoirs. Chittor Fort is famous for its three legendary sieges, including the acts of jauhar (self-immolation) by its women to avoid capture. Notable landmarks include the Tower of Victory (Vijay Stambh) and the Tower of Fame (Kirti Stambh). The fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and symbolizes Rajput valor and sacrifice.

Things to do:

1.Watch the evening Light and Sound show

2.Marvel at the Jain Temples and Meera Temples

3.Explore the countless reservoirs

4.Admire the grandeur of Padmini Palace, Vijay Stambh and Kirti Stambh

Timings : 9:45 AM – 6:30 PM

Panhala Fort

Panhala Fort

Panhala Fort, located near Kolhapur in Maharashtra, is one of the largest forts in the Deccan region. Built in the 12th century by the Shilahara dynasty, it later became an important stronghold for the Marathas. The fort is closely linked with the Maratha king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who used it strategically during his battles against the Mughals. Panhala Fort features massive fortifications, secret passageways, and important structures like the Sajja Kothi and Ambarkhana granaries. The fort's elevated location offers panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain range, and it played a significant role in Maratha history.

Junagarh Fort

Junagarh Fort

Junagarh Fort, located in Bikaner, Rajasthan, is an impressive fort known for never being conquered. Built in 1594 by Raja Rai Singh, a general in the Mughal army, the fort showcases a blend of Mughal, Rajput, and Gujarati architecture. It features massive walls, grand courtyards, and ornate palaces such as Anup Mahal and Phool Mahal, adorned with intricate carvings and frescoes. The fort also houses temples and a museum displaying royal artifacts. Unlike many forts in Rajasthan, Junagarh is situated on a plain rather than a hill, highlighting its strong defensive design.

Things to do:

1.Admire the stunning architecture at Junagarh Fort

2.Enjoy the camel safari at the Thar Desert

3.Go for shopping at the Kote Gate

Best Time to Visit :November to February

Address : Junagarh Fort Road, Bikaner, Rajasthan – 334001

Timings : 10:00 AM – 4:30 PM

Entry Fee : INR 50 (Indian Nationals), INR 300 (Foreign Nationals)
